Eternal Gods

Eternal gods are something whose existence and power is undeniable, they don’t require prayer to enforce their power or existence and very rarely answers any. An example of an eternal god is the Soln and Lunre, which are the sun and moon in Mihridahl. The eternal gods maintain the reality and natural order, they also maintain the barrier that exists between the realms so that one realm would not affect the other too much. Because of the way the eternal gods work it is often presumed that they are unaware of the existence of other gods or creatures and simply do their work.

Immortal Gods

Immortal gods gain power from their worshippers, while they have a lot of power they do not exist in any other realm than their own, instead they use peoples prayer to create a connection to other realms and enforce their existence there allowing them to use their powers to affect it. Even if they have no worshippers they still exist but only in their own realm unable to affect other realms. In Athernis, thought is such a strong nature force that it can manifest itself into reality, the Eternal gods don’t hold any actual physical body but rather is a strong thought and soul willed into reality, unlike demons this reality can only exist in that form on Athernis and would scatter the moment it set foot in most other realms apart from Devorna. The only place they would actually be able to continue to exist in is Devorna but only as a vessel to a demonic essence. An eternal god sees itself as the perfect being and wishes others to be as perfect as them, this leads the gods to try and influence other races to become more like them and ultimately make other races exactly like them in body and mind.

Mortal Gods

Mortal gods unlike Eternals can exist in any realm and while their powers may not be as great as the Eternals they are very powerful compared to any other living creature. A mortal god has a mortal body which means that they are killable. Unlike Eternal gods, mortal gods do not need to follow a certain portfolio but often they can gain a title based on their actions, such as if a mortal god shows its power in war it can be worshipped as a god of war.
